========================================================================================================================
WHY COVERAGE IS 25.2%
========================================================================================================================

1. METRIC AVAILABILITY BY ERA:
   - Many metrics only tracked from 2008-2020 (e.g., broadband, co2_emissions)
   - Some metrics discontinued (e.g., internet_hosts stopped in 2017)
   - Early editions (2000-2006) had fewer metrics

2. COUNTRY COVERAGE GAPS:
   - Small nations often have incomplete data
   - Conflict zones have reporting gaps
   - Some territories included in factbook but with minimal data

3. DATA YEAR vs FACTBOOK EDITION:
   - Each factbook edition reports data from MULTIPLE prior years
   - A 2020 edition might have 2018 data for GDP but 2019 data for population
   - This creates sparse coverage across the year dimension

4. EXPECTED vs THEORETICAL:
   - 255 countries x 31 years = 7,905 theoretical max per metric
   - But many metrics weren't tracked in early years
   - Realistic max is much lower for most metrics

========================================================================================================================
CONCLUSION
========================================================================================================================

25.2% overall coverage is reasonable given:
- Variable metric availability across 21 factbook editions
- Many metrics only introduced in later editions
- Geographic and demographic data has best coverage (40-50%)
- Specialized financial/environmental data has lowest coverage (<15%)

The data extraction pipeline is working correctly. Low coverage reflects
the actual data availability in the source World Factbook HTML files.
